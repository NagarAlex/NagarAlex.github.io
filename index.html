Introduction

This is the very rudimentary foundation of my website. I am Alex Nagar. I am an economist by training but really an economic historian these days. I have just completed my DPhil in Economics at the University of Oxford this year. I am not "on the job market" in the sense of looking for a tenure track role, but I am looking for postdocs at the moment.

See here for my research. Long ago I decided that my comparative advantage was not in the "X and Y: Evidence from Z" type of (economist-style) economic history, even if that can often be valuable work. Instead, I focus on collecting data from a wide range of sources to get consistent long term time series and use these alongside economic theory to explore the past, much more like a traditional quantitative economic historian. One advantage of that strategy is that I can examine the big picture, and questions, rather than well-identified trivialities. In this sense, I have betrayed my educational background, even if I still think what I do is actually properly still economics.

Research Interests

These are the keywords (as is customary) that summarize my research interests: Economic History, Institutions, Technical Change, Agriculture, Industrialization (or lack thereof) in the "Periphery", Traditional Industries, Monetary History.

My DPhil work has focused on the Indian subcontinent, but I'm also interested in the rest of Asia.
